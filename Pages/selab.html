A Shift in Pedagogy

This framework shifts the lab's focus from producing isolated documents to an integrated, value-driven process that mirrors modern industry practices. The goal is to cultivate an Agile mindset: embracing change, prioritizing collaboration, and delivering working software incrementally.

Traditional "Waterfall" Approach

  • Linear, sequential phases.
  • Focus on comprehensive upfront documentation (SRS).
  • Change is seen as a problem to be avoided.
  • Lacks modern version control (Git).
  • Assessment is heavily based on artifacts.
  • Prepares for an outdated development model.

Proposed "Agile" Approach

  • Iterative, cyclical development (Sprints).
  • Focus on working software as the measure of progress.
  • Change is welcomed as valuable feedback.
  • Built on modern tooling (Git, Project Boards).
  • Assessment balances product and process.
  • Prepares students for current industry practices.

Assessment & Evaluation Strategy

The 60/40 evaluation scheme is designed to reward both the final product and the engineering process. This ensures students are graded on collaboration, quality, and adherence to modern practices.

Overall Mark Distribution

Internal Assessment Breakdown (60 Marks)

A. Sprint Deliverables (30): Quality of working software demonstrated in each sprint review.

B. Process & Quality (20): Quality of Git history, code reviews, project board management, and effective use of the AI Scrum Master.

C. Individual & Demo (10): Based on confidential peer evaluation and the final project presentation.
